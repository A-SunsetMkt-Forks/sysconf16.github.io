Building a PC

Prerequisites

  1. Lay the parts on a large surface (e.g. a table)
  2. Ground yourself by touching a large, metal object (e.g. a radiator)

Motherboard Preparation

  1. Open the motherboard box and remove the motherboard from its anti-static bag
  2. Close the motherboard box and place the motherboard on top
  3. Open the CPU box and remove the CPU tray
  4. Open the CPU socket by lifting the lever up and to the opposite side until you hear a click if on an AMD platform, or by pushing the latch down, out then lifting to the opposite side if on an Intel platform
  5. Open the CPU tray and remove the CPU, holding it by the edges to not touch the pads or pins on the underside
  6. Insert the CPU into the socket by aligning the arrow or dot on the socket with the arrow printed on the CPU
  7. Close the socket in the reverse way it was opened. There may be some resistance if installing an Intel CPU
  8. Remove the RAM from its packaging
  9. Refer to the motherboard's manual to choose the correct slots to insert the DIMMs to enable the dual channel memory mode
  10. Open the latches on the sides of the DIMM slots on the motherboard
  11. Align the notch in the centre of the DIMM with the notch in the centre of the slot in the motherboard
  12. Insert each stick one by one, pushing firmly on the edges of the stick to seat it within the slot, listening for a click to indicate successful installation
  13. If installing an M.2 drive, remove it from the packaging
  14. Refer to the motherboard's manual to choose the correct M.2 slot to install the drive to ensure correct compatibility (e.g. if a PCIe Gen 4 drive is being used, ensure that the drive is being installed into a PCIe Gen 4 compliant M.2 slot)
  15. Unscrew the M.2 screw from the stand-off or if not pre-installed, find the M.2 screw in the motherboard box
  16. Slide the pins of the M.2 drive into the slot with the label facing outwards
  17. If the screw hole of the M.2 drive does not align with the stand-off, remove the drive and move the stand-off to a different hole on the board
  18. Screw the M.2 drive into the slot
  19. If using a stock or aftermarket air cooler, remove the cooler from its packaging
  20. If using a liquid cooler, leave it in the packaging for now and skip the following 2 steps
  21. Follow the instructions provided with the cooler to ensure proper installation as each cooler will differ in technique
  22. If thermal paste is not pre-applied to the cooler, apply aftermarket thermal paste by removing the syringe cap and pushing a pea-sized blob onto the centre of the CPU

Case Preparation

  1. Remove the case from its packaging and remove the accessory box
  2. Unscrew and remove both side-panels by unscrewing the thumbscrews on the side or rear of the side-panel depending on case model
  3. Remove the front-panel from the case if additional fans are going to be installed
  4. Remove the accessory box from the case
  5. Remove any additional fans from their packaging and screw them into the case using the included self-tapping screws
  6. Lay the case on its side
  7. If the motherboard does not have a pre-installed I/O shield, re-open the motherboard box and remove the I/O shield
  8. Remove the I/O shield from its packet and install it in the correct orientation at the rear of the case
  9. Open the case's accessories box and remove the screw packet
  10. Open the screw packet and pour the screws into a container

Motherboard Mounting

  1. Test-fit the motherboard into the case to ensure that all of the pre-installed motherboard stand-offs in the case align with the screw holes on the motherboard
  2. If the test-fit is not successful, first use pliers to remove any misplaced stand-offs, then install them into the correct locations on the motherboard tray
  3. If further stand-offs are required, sift through the screws in the container
  4. Re-fit the motherboard into the case ensuring that the rear I/O aligns with the I/O shield
  5. Use the curved circular screws to screw the motherboard into the motherboard tray
  6. If using a liquid cooler, remove it from its packaging
  7. Mount the radiator to the case. It is recommended to install this at the top, ensuring that the fans are facing inside the case to push air outwards
  8. Follow the instructions provided with the cooler to ensure proper installation of the waterblock. Each cooler will differ in technique

Drives, Expansion Cards & Power Supply

  1. Refer to the motherboard manual's front-panel connector layout and install the case front-panel connectors (power, reset, power LED, disk activity LED, front-panel audio and USB) to the motherboard
  2. Open and mount any 2.5" or 3.5" drives to the drive bays using the hexagonal-shaped headed screws
  3. If a 2.5" or 3.5" drive has been mounted, remove a SATA cable from the motherboard box and connect the drive to the motherboard's SATA ports
  4. Remove the PSU from its packaging
  5. Insert the PSU into the case and mount it inside the case using the included screws
  6. Connect the 24-pin motherboard power cable from the PSU to the right side of the motherboard
  7. Connect the 8-pin CPU EPS power cable from the PSU to the top left of the motherboard
  8. If your motherboard only has a 4-pin EPS power connector, split the 8-pin from the PSU into two halves and insert the half which fits
  9. Connect the SATA power cables from the PSU to any installed 2.5" or 3.5" drives
  10. If installing a discrete graphics card, remove it from its packaging
  11. Remove the PCI blanking plates from the case corresponding to the 16X slot, and any additional depending on GPU width
  12. Remove the plastic cover over the PCI-E 16X connector on the graphics card if present
  13. Push the GPU into the motherboard's PCI-E 16X slot
  14. Screw the graphics card into the case using the hexagonal-shaped headed screws
  15. Connect any PCI-E peripheral power connectors from the PSU to the GPU if required, splitting the 8-pin connector apart if only 6-pins are required
  16. If installing a Wi-Fi or Bluetooth card, remove it from its packaging
  17. Remove the PCI blanking plate from the case corresponding to the 1x slot into which the card is to be installed on the motherboard
  18. Insert the card into the PCI-E 1x slot of the motherboard
  19. Screw the Wi-Fi or Bluetooth card into the case using the hexagonal-shaped headed screws

Finishing Touches

  1. Using zip ties or velcro straps, tie down as many cables as possible at the rear of the motherboard tray
  2. Re-install the front and side-panels in the reverse way by which they were removed
  3. Connect a mouse, keyboard and monitor to the computer, ensuring to plug the monitor into the graphics card and not the motherboard if a discrete GPU is being used
  4. Remove the kettle lead from the PSU box and plug the motherboard into the mains
  5. Flick the switch on the back of the PSU to the on position and press the power button
  6. Wait for the PC to POST, while repeatedly pressing the BIOS key on the keyboard to enter the BIOS. This may take up to a minute on first boot. See here for a list of BIOS keys
  7. Verify that the CPU, RAM, and all drives are correctly detected
  8. Shut down the PC by holding the power button
